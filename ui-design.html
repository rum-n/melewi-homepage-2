  • What is UI design?

    UI stands for user interface. This means everything your users will see when they open your website, web app or mobile app. Colors, shapes, fonts, visual elements, and the overall style are things that fall into the category of UI.
  • What is the difference between UI design and UX design?
    Simply put, UX design deals with how the website, web or mobile app is structured and the UI design determines how it looks like.
  • Do I need both UX & UI design?
    It really depends what your goals are. If you're creating a product from scratch it's imperative that you do both UX & UI design. If you already have an existing product and you're happy with your user flows, but your looks are outdated, you can only do a UI redesign to keep things up-to-date. As a general rule, designs become outdated every 4-5 years.
  • What is the UI design process?
    As with UX design, the UI design process consists of an initial meeting to get our team alligned with what needs to be designed. When coming up with designs for your user interface, it's important for us to know who your users are and what do they expect to see. We consider your branding and the emotions you need to convey in your users when they open your product. After we have all that figured, we start creating our designs, showing you our progress during frequent client review meetings.
  • Why is UI important?
    Since the dawn of mankind, people have appreciated good aestethics. Perceptions have changed and will continue to change, but there will always be a dinstinction between good and bad design, and people will always favor things that contain the former.
